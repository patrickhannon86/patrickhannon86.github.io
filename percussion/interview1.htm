Interview With Elizabeth

March 30th, 2003:  Elizabeth Tuazon, a.k.a. Tarzan

          Tarzan has played numerous instruments over the course of her years, and these include the piano, bass drum "2," concert percussion, marching snare, and her own voice in singing.  She has been playing the piano for twelve years to this date, she played bass drum for one, snare drum for three years, concert percussion for eight years, and has been singing for six years.  In her opinion, the instrument that has the greatest influence in her life so far has been the piano, as she has been playing it the longest, and is trying out for all her college auditions on. (Elizabeth is currently attending Illinois Wesleyan University -ed)  Many a multitude of experiences have left a lasting impression on her.  "Drumline," she says, "has made me a lot more vulgar."  To say the least, I'm sure.  Marching band throughout her experience in it has given her a "great and ever-growing respect" of Mr. Romano.  As she has gotten to work closer with him in recent years, she noted that she has seen all the hard work and effort he puts into his work, and holds him in the utmost respect for what he has done and continues to do.  Being a section leader has given Tarzan the opportunity to convey her opinions and thoughts much more easily, and has expanded her responsibility greatly.  "You've got to have confidence in yourself if you're going to accomplish anything, it's just common sense," she conveyed. 

           The drumline has established countless traditions over the years, some out of common sense, others out of enjoyment.  One of the staples of the Alan Barone regime has been not to say verbals.  "I guess that's always been one of Alan's things, not doing verbals," Tarzan pontificated.  Another tradition of the drumline is to participate in our own separate warm-ups, simply because we do things differently than the band.  Also included are Jeeves, and the "rooster" Dante.  One other tradition of warm-up setup, is "V-ing it up," or "Arcing it up," placing our hats in front of our warm-up arc.  Naturally, what would experiences be without stories?  There are quite a few of those as are with any occurrence in history.  Tarzan has discussed with me a few of these stories, which I shall presently relate.  At Lake Park, the first competition of the season, right before the gates onto the track, Kevin Durnbaugh laid his hand upon one of the fence posts.  When he took off his hand, the fencepost top was no longer there, it was on his hand.  Ever since then it has been celebrated as a victory for us over Lake Park, because the fencepost is gone, we have it.  Durnbaugh was from thereon thought of as the drumline "good luck charm."  Going off subject here, I would just like to add an interesting quote from Tarzan, "Phil played tenors, we all wonder what's going to happen next year, everyone." (Psychic ability, perhaps? -ed)  Interesting thought to ponder, eh?  Moving on, Tarzan's sophomore year, right before state, she was playing very badly and not in time.  Kevin Durnbaugh hypothesized that it was the bass drums causing her to screw up.  So, he switched with her, and, finding they were indeed the problem, stayed there, much to her surprise.  They re-learned all their drill a few weeks before state, which "was scary," as Tarzan put it, but in the end everything was much cleaner.  "Though Tony can't march, he was an inherently good bass drummer."  And surprisingly enough, Tarzan originally wanted to be in the pit, because of her piano experience.  However, today she couldn't possibly see herself there. 

             Tarzan's band mother was Michelle Krichbaumpt.  She was "generally a good mom," to Tarzan, though being in Pit, Tarzan and her relative didn't interact that much at rehearsal.  Marching band, in her opinion, is so physically hard in the beginning, yet once it is mastered is so much fun.  Tarzan's child is Jim Coneybear, and she confesses that she is a terrible mom, mainly because of Jim being Jim...  Michael Guptwa, in Tarzan's opinion was one of the more vulgar drummers, and swore profusely in German.  There was a trio of obscene and vulgar visual-creators.  This trio included Kevin Durnbaugh, Scott Fawkes, and Mike Guptwa.  Kevin created the Heroin and the blood of Christ visual, which I will cover more extensively in a moment.  Scott Fawkes was famous for his abortion visual.  Moving back to the blood, One day Kevin had a cut on his wrist, and Tarzan and him were joking about it, somehow connecting it to Jesus.  He took his sticks and made a cross with the blood, held it up, and yelled, "The blood of Christ!"  So it was born, "Periodically," Tarzan says.  One of the other Durnbaugh incidents was the praying mantis stick "thing."  There are so many other experiences she has come to know, one of which that "made her season" freshman year.  Of course, I could delve deeper into all four years of her life in high school, but then she claimed that I would own her soul...  Godspeed.   -JS
