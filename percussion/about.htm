About Us

     Drumline History

      The Victor J. Andrew Drumline was established as the percussion complement to the marching band during the fall. Numerous accomplished instructors have undertaken positions in instructing the drumline in the past, such as Mr. Pat Slowick and others. The marching band has previously attained the status of state champions at the Illinois State University invitational in 1994, 1997, and 2002. This past fall season, in 2003 the band took 2nd place in class 5A, with one of the caption awards being best percussion in class 5A.

      As the focus of the drumline shifted from being not only a complement to the band, but a fully functioning separate ensemble, the winter percussion group was born. As the ensemble has grown, in 2003 the group traveled to the WGI Midwest Championships in Dayton, Ohio performing their show entitled "Medea". This year, in 2004, the ensemble has moved from the Scholastic A Class to Scholastic Open Class performing their show, "Music from 311".  The songs highlighted from this show were "Do You Right," "Beyond the Grey Sky," and "Unity." The ensemble strives to continue to advance individually and ensembly towards more challenging shows.

     The instructional staff for the drumline has changed dramatically over the years to include numerous instructors. In their fourth year instructing the Victor J. Andrew Drumline, with Mr. Alan Barone and Mr. Nick Braasch instructing the drumline, and Mr. Mike Zuziak instructing the pit, the drumline has had the benefit of great professionals in music composition and technique, among their other numerous qualities.  This summer we have had the pleasure of Mr. Mike Gucwa coming to instruct the tenors, and we've greatly appreciated his help.   Mr. Scott Fawkes has also helped out the snares during rehearsals, thanks again.  During the winter, Mr. Jason Kulak assists with both the pit, and the marching and visual aspects concerning the drumline.   Many alumni of the drumline also come to see the current members perform.

     The drumline has always stressed the principle of getting good, while having fun achieving this goal. By achieving what needs to get done, one can have fun while doing it.  Also, maintaining tradition is a fundamental aspect, such as with Jeeves and stretches.  Along with these traditions is the tradition of goodwill at rehearsals.  Speaking of traditions, tradition is a highly respected tradition, in the traditional sense; traditionally, mind you...
    
      Many members of the drumline and front ensemble traveled with the marching band this past spring to Florida, the tri-annual trip.  They participated in the parades in both the Magic Kingdom and MGM Studios.  Other percussionists traveled with the concert and jazz bands and performed with their ensembles in the Orlando Music-fest Competition at a local college.  The band took first place in the marching competitions, as did the winter percussion ensemble.  The jazz band took second in its class and the two concert bands 5th, and 6th, respectively.
